## Core Position

流浪的蛤蟆's advice is useful because it is brutally operational. He separates literary ambition, web-novel performance, platform survival, and the writer's current ability.

Core rule:

```text
Write inside your current ability, but train by reading and writing above your current ability.
Do not confuse improving prose, improving web-novel results, and satisfying personal taste.
```

What this means for my fiction:

- Before asking "is the prose good," first ask whether the story has market-facing clarity, reader pull, a main character, and an executable long goal.
- Do not use "I want to write my own thing" as an excuse for ignoring reader demand.
- Do not use "market" as an excuse for writing without taste. Find the overlap between what I like and what readers want.

## 1. Classic Reading: Improve Prose, Not Scores

He distinguishes improving writing level from improving web-novel performance:

- To improve Chinese prose and craft depth, read traditional Chinese fiction, strong modern Chinese prose, and professional background materials.
- To improve web-novel results, read hot web novels and study their technique, rhythm, and reader promise.
- Writing-technique books are more useful after the writer has some practice; beginners often need reading volume, finished drafts, and better concepts first.

Recommended learning direction from the answers:

- Chinese genre/classic material: 还珠楼主, 金庸, 古龙, 梁羽生, 温瑞安, 黄易, 四大名著, `封神演义`, `镜花缘`, `济公传` family, `三言二拍`, `聊斋`.
- Foreign high-utility story material: Jules Verne, Mark Twain, Alexandre Dumas.
- For late-Qing/Republican-era prose force, he points to works where classical Chinese grounding met modern narrative methods.

What this means for my fiction:

- Read classics with a target: battle, dialogue, daily scene, character entrance, quest, apprenticeship, first encounter, artifact use, or social pressure.
- Do not imitate antique wording in publishable web-novel prose. Translate the lesson into modern clarity.
- "杂书多" is not decoration. It is the reservoir that lets a writer combine story boxes, tone, background, and details.

## 2. Story Boxes And Purposeful Reading

Several answers repeat the same principle: story cores are limited, but variations are endless.

Examples of story boxes:

- first encounter / first love
- apprenticeship
- gaining power without a teacher
- entering a secret organization
- revenge trigger
- public humiliation and payoff
- rescue
- hidden identity
- quest for a resource
- small goal inside a large goal

What this means for my fiction:

- When I cannot invent plot, I should not wait for inspiration. Pick a story box and collect many versions.
- For each version, record: who wants what, what blocks them, what method is used, what detail makes it memorable, and how it exits into the next problem.
- A new story often comes from recombining familiar boxes with a new motive, setting, rule, or moral pressure.

## 3. Satisfaction Is Compensation After Pressure

His answer on 爽文 is especially useful: the same reward is not equally satisfying for every character. Satisfaction depends on prior lack, humiliation, desire, danger, and cost.

What this means for my fiction:

- A reward is satisfying only if the reader understands why this specific person needs it.
- 打脸 works only after the opponent is made memorable and emotionally deserving of defeat.
- Beauty, money, status, victory, or power must be attached to pressure. Without pressure, payoff is decoration.
- A scene can be "爽到哭" when it combines injustice, helplessness, effort, family/emotional stakes, and sudden compensation.

Satisfaction checklist:

```text
what does the protagonist lack:
who caused pressure or humiliation:
why does the reader care:
what exact payoff repairs the wound:
what public recognition or private relief follows:
what new problem appears after the payoff:
```

## 4. Combat Is About Ideology And Setup

In his combat answers, the important point is not choreography. A fight is satisfying when the reader wants the protagonist to win.

What this means for my fiction:

- Before writing a fight, write why the opponent deserves defeat.
- A fight may need several chapters of setup if it is meant to be emotionally explosive.
- Learn from `水浒传`: some classic fights are not technically complex, but they are narratively powerful because the moral pressure is clear.
- For fantasy combat, study the best available examples by extracting every fight scene and comparing how stakes, ability, distance, rhythm, and payoff work.


## 5. Long-Form Planning: Goals Beat Vague Ambition

He repeatedly warns against writing without a long target. A full outline is not always required, but major word-count blocks need clear goals.

What this means for my fiction:

- For every major segment, set a concrete target. Example form: by 200k words, apprenticeship; by 400k, first spell/skill complete; by 600k, first mission.
- Long novels can use maps, levels, organizations, and power tiers to reset pressure, but "looping maps" is hard and must still feel fresh.
- If the novel collapses, sometimes the answer is not to over-polish. Finish the book or finish the arc to train completion ability.

## 6. Revision And Practice

His "越改越烂" answer treats revision as an ability unlocked by practice. A beginner can rewrite at the same level forever if the underlying ability has not improved.

What this means for my fiction:

- Do not endlessly polish chapter one at level one. Write forward, accumulate experience, then revise with new ability.
- Record what feels wrong, but do not stop the whole project every time a sentence disappoints me.
- Practice must include finishing, not only beginning.
- Fanfic or imitation practice can expose real gaps. The point is self-knowledge, not publishable imitation.

## 7. Market And Platform Sense

Many answers are not about prose; they are about adult reality in web fiction:

- Know the platform, update expectations, signing rules, policy risks, editor communication norms, and reader taste.
- Editors prefer efficient communication: say the issue directly.
- A signed but quiet book does not automatically deserve attention; many people are writing harder and longer.
- Follow trends only if you understand what is actually hot. Many beginners think they are following a trend but are actually following a dead pattern.
- The overlap of personal interest and market demand is the best starting point.

What this means for my fiction:

- If writing for Qidian or another platform, the opening must show the protagonist, genre, pressure, and promise quickly.
- Project planning should include market fit and update discipline, not only plot imagination.
- Do not mistake obscure personal taste for originality.

## 8. Materials, Notes, And Reading Habits

His advice is blunt: there is no clever substitute for reading a lot.

What this means for my fiction:

- Build a reading notebook by story box and technique, not by vague impressions.
- Scan hot new books even if they are not personal favorites; learn what readers are rewarding.
- Keep private reading for pleasure separate from professional reading for technique.
- When source materials pile up, convert them into plot or scene tasks. Unused research is not progress.

## 9. Setting And Copyright

His advice on infinite-flow and public-domain materials is directly useful:

- Avoid building stories on unauthorized modern IP if publication or adaptation matters.
- Public-domain classics, mythology, and original worlds are safer.
- If using familiar material, transform it into a new mechanism instead of relying on the borrowed shell.

What this means for my fiction:

- For game-world or infinite-flow ideas, prefer original instances, public-domain inspiration, or explicitly authorized material.
- Every borrowed mechanism must become new character pressure and new event logic.
