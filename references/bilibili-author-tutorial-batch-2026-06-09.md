## 1. Three-Layer Story Thinking

The strongest lesson from `写作原理.docx` is to stop treating "story" as one vague thing. Use three layers.

| Layer | Question | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Macro story | What is this long novel for, and what broad journey does it promise? | theme, genre promise, phases, world rules, main cast, long conflict |
| Meso plot unit | What complete unit of desire and conflict is being executed now? | emotional incident, goal, obstacle, method, action/climax/turn, feedback |
| Micro event | How does the reader experience this exact scene? | viewpoint, information, action, detail, emotion curve, pacing, hook |

What this means for my fiction:

- When a chapter feels shapeless, identify which layer is missing. A chapter can fail because the macro promise is vague, the plot unit has no obstacle, or the micro scene has no readable information/emotion.
- Before drafting, write one sentence for each layer: long promise, local plot unit, immediate scene experience.
- Do not let an outline be only event order. It must also say what the reader should want, fear, doubt, or expect.

## 2. Macro Story Checklist

A long web novel is a large story made of many smaller stories. Each smaller story should have its own purpose while still pushing the large direction.

Macro planning fields:

```text
core theme / reader promise:
genre and target emotion:
main protagonist desire:
main opposition or pressure system:
world material: nature, resources, map, society, rules, powers/tools:
major phases:
phase promises:
recurring reader expectations:
long-term payoff:
```

What this means for my fiction:

- If I only have a setting or golden finger, I must convert it into a protagonist desire and phase ladder.
- If I only have a theme, I must choose the world, people, and conflict that can make the theme happen in scenes.
- Each volume/arc needs a local theme and payoff, but it cannot drift away from the main reader promise.

## 3. Meso Plot Unit: Six-Step Engine

For a usable plot unit, build this chain:

```text
emotional incident
-> desire target
-> obstacle / dilemma
-> method / resource / plan
-> action resolution with climax or turn
-> feedback / reward / new problem
```

What this means for my fiction:

- A plot does not start because "something happens"; it starts because an event creates desire, pressure, or emotional need.
- Obstacles are not decoration. They make the eventual payoff stronger because the reader has watched cost accumulate.
- A turn should be logical but not expected. If it has no setup, it feels random. If it only confirms expectation, it is flat.
- Feedback is mandatory. After the event, the protagonist, relationship, status, resource, secret, or reader question must change.

## 4. Reader Desire And Expectation

The tutorials separate reader pull into three useful forces:

- desire substitution: the protagonist carries a wish the reader can borrow
- emotional experience: the reader wants anger, fear, sweetness, relief, awe, comedy, revenge, discovery, or warmth
- expectation: the reader wants to know what happens, how it happens, whether it succeeds, or when the promised payoff arrives

What this means for my fiction:

- Each chapter should name its dominant reader pull. Do not ask one scene to do every emotion at once.
- A "cool idea" is not enough. It must become a desire, an emotional state, or a question with delayed payoff.
- When revising, mark which paragraphs increase expectation and which paragraphs only explain. Too much explanation without expectation becomes drag.

## 5. Micro Event Execution

A scene becomes readable through three controls:

```text
viewpoint -> what can be known
information -> what is revealed, hidden, delayed, or misunderstood
emotion -> how pressure rises, drops, reverses, and pays off
```

Practical scene fields:

```text
POV:
POV knowledge boundary:
reader knows more / less / same:
visible action:
hidden intention:
important object or detail:
scene emotion at start:
scene emotion at end:
what changed:
next hook:
```

What this means for my fiction:

- Unknown is not the same as confusion. The reader must understand the scene well enough to wonder about the missing piece.
- Information gaps can exist between characters, between reader and character, or between current scene and hidden backstory.
- If I change POV, I need a reason: better suspense, better irony, better emotional contrast, or clearer information.

## 6. Opening As A Reader Contract

`大师作品开头.docx` shows that the first page tells the reader how to read the whole work.

Opening contracts can include:

- concrete realism: a small action reveals poverty, pressure, duty, humiliation, or dignity
- unreliable self-narration: the narrator's tone creates charm and doubt at the same time
- sensory/dream logic: sound, motion, image, and association establish a strange or poetic mode
- commercial hook: title, synopsis, crisis, advantage, and future payoff become visible fast

What this means for my fiction:

- Chapter one should not only start the plot; it should establish the book's reading method.
- Choose one detail that does at least three jobs: world, character, pressure, theme, mood, or future question.
- For web novels, do not imitate literary opacity blindly. Use the mechanism: make the opening detail functional and legible.
- If the story later depends on fractured memory, unreliable narration, public humiliation, hidden poverty, or symbolic objects, seed the method early.

## 7. Ordinary Idea Expansion

`发散性思考和细节深化.docx` gives a practical way to develop a plain idea.

Start with five questions:

```text
Who is the protagonist?
What do they want right now?
What stops them?
What is the price of failure?
Why must the story start now?
```

Then find head and tail:

```text
inciting incident -> climax/confrontation
```

After that, branch from concrete conditions:

- What can happen in this place?
- Who can interrupt?
- What object/tool/disguise/exit/resource exists here?
- What misunderstanding can change the pressure?
- What obligation can force the protagonist to sacrifice the original goal?
- What earlier detail can become the ending callback?

What this means for my fiction:

- I do not need to wait for a perfect idea. I can force a weak idea to reveal story potential through pressure, price, and moral choice.
- The best branch is not always the loudest. Choose the branch that changes the protagonist's decision or exposes character.
- The ending should not merely stop. It should pay off a planted detail, reveal a hidden connection, or show changed relationship/status.

## 8. AI Long-Form Writing Workflow

`AI自动生成小说正文.docx` confirms the engineering rule already used by this skill: long novels need project files, not one giant prompt.

Use this loop:

```text
story bible
-> core setting
-> volume outline
-> unit outline
-> chapter outline / task card
-> Draft A
-> review for logic, continuity, market promise, and readability
-> Draft B
-> de-AI and naturalization pass
-> continuity update
```

What this means for my fiction:

- Never ask the AI to continue blindly. Give it the relevant bible, previous-chapter handoff, current task card, forbidden changes, and target reader effect.
- AI self-review is a first filter, not final truth. Human taste, user feedback, and reader/market response outrank the model's self-score.
- If a draft goes wrong, repair the framework before rewriting prose. Many prose problems are actually outline, information, or character-boundary problems.

## 9. Qidian Opening Supplement

`签约作家的个人经验与心得.docx` overlaps the 2026-06-08 signing transcript, but it reinforces several Qidian-facing checks.

Use it as an opening audit:

```text
title: genre and contradiction visible?
synopsis: protagonist, crisis, advantage, route visible?
first 500 words: time, place, protagonist state, pressure visible?
first 6000 words: hook, conflict, crisis, advantage, reader question visible?
first 3 chapters: focus stays close to protagonist?
golden finger: solves an immediate pressure and promises later payoff?
language: simple, direct, no decorative fog?
```

What this means for my fiction:

- Opening prose should be clear before it is clever.
- A crisis must force movement. If the protagonist can ignore it, the opening loses commercial pressure.
- A golden finger or advantage should not float in the background; it should answer a visible problem and create expectation for a future public payoff.

## 10. Chapter Task Card Additions

When drafting a new chapter, add these fields to the usual task card if the chapter feels weak:

```text
macro promise served:
meso plot-unit step:
reader desire being activated:
information gap:
opening/detail seed:
pressure branch:
emotion low point:
emotion high point:
callback or payoff:
framework risk:
```

What this means for my fiction:

- These fields stop the AI from writing "events in order" without reader effect.
- They are especially useful for opening chapters, weak middle chapters, and AI-generated drafts that feel generic.

## 11. Revision Checks

Before accepting Draft B or Draft C:

- Does the chapter have purpose, structure, material, and character desire?
- Does each major scene have a visible goal, obstacle, price, and changed result?
- Does the reader know enough to follow, but not so much that all expectation dies?
- Is the opening detail doing more than decoration?
- Are emotional lows and highs arranged instead of random?
- Did the AI preserve continuity from project files?
- Did the de-AI pass remove generic phrasing without damaging clarity?

What this means for my fiction:

- Revision should not only polish sentences. It should test whether the chapter actually moves desire, information, emotion, and plot.
- If the draft is boring, first repair the engine, not the adjectives.
