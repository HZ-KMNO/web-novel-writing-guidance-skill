# De-AI Polishing

Use this only after content direction is approved, unless the user explicitly asks for a style-only pass.

## Principle

De-AI polishing changes expression, not canon.

It may improve:

- rhythm
- detail
- dialogue
- sensory texture
- implication
- paragraphing
- emotional embodiment

It must preserve:

- events
- outcomes
- who knows what
- character goals
- setting rules
- foreshadowing
- timeline
- relationship state

## Common AI Flavor

Watch for:

- stock phrases
- symmetrical paragraphing
- over-explained emotions
- abstract mood labels
- generic atmosphere
- moral summary at emotional peaks
- dialogue that explains plot too cleanly
- characters saying exactly what they feel
- every beat being too polished
- repeated sentence structures
- scene endings that summarize instead of change

## Fix Patterns

### Replace Abstract Emotion

Instead of:

```text
She felt very sad and disappointed.
```

Use:

```text
She folded the letter twice, then once more, though the crease had already gone white.
```

### Cut Explanation

If the scene already shows a feeling, remove the explanatory sentence.

Bad pattern:

```text
He clenched his fist. He was furious because he had been betrayed.
```

Better:

```text
He closed his hand around the cup until the tea trembled against the rim.
```

### Make Dialogue Less Direct

Characters rarely state everything.

Use:

- interruption
- evasion
- subtext
- status pressure
- unfinished thoughts
- contradiction between words and action

### Add Lived-In Detail

Good detail should do work:

- reveal class, habit, fear, desire, danger, or relationship
- make the setting visible
- show what the character notices
- imply backstory without explaining it

Avoid decorative detail that does not affect mood, action, or understanding.

### Vary Rhythm

Mix:

- short pressure sentences
- medium action sentences
- longer reflective sentences when the character has space to think
- paragraph breaks at choice, reveal, or emotional turn

Do not make every paragraph the same length.

## Dialogue Humanization Checklist

Check:

- Does each speaker have a distinct goal?
- Does word choice reflect their status and personality?
- Is any line only there to explain lore?
- Can a line be cut and replaced with action?
- Is silence more effective than a full answer?
- Does the conversation change the situation?

## Scene Ending

End on:

- a decision
- a cost
- a reveal
- a changed relationship
- a physical action
- a new question
- a concrete image that carries consequence

Avoid ending on:

- "and he knew everything had changed"
- "she realized she had to be stronger"
- generic emotional summary

## Final Pass Checklist

Before returning Draft C:

- No major new plot events added.
- No canon facts changed.
- No important user preference erased.
- Generic phrases reduced.
- Dialogue feels character-driven.
- Emotion appears through behavior.
- Paragraph rhythm varies.
- Final continuity record can still match the chapter.
