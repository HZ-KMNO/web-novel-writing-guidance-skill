# Quality Supervision

Use this reference when reviewing, diagnosing, revising, or approving fiction output.

## Review Order

Always review from structural to stylistic:

1. Reader promise
2. Chapter purpose
3. Protagonist goal
4. Conflict and pressure
5. Situation change
6. Continuity
7. Character motivation
8. Pacing
9. Scene clarity
10. Hook and payoff
11. Dialogue
12. Prose
13. De-AI residue

Do not polish prose while the chapter has no purpose.

## Story Engine Gate

Pass when:

- The protagonist wants something.
- The obstacle directly blocks that desire.
- Failure has a price.
- The story begins now for a reason.
- The reader can identify what pleasure the genre promises.

Failure signs:

- The idea is only a setting.
- The protagonist is only observing.
- No one loses anything if the chapter stops.
- The story could start on any day.

## Chapter Gate

Pass when:

- The chapter starts with inherited pressure or a clear new pressure.
- At least one concrete goal is pursued.
- Opposition appears.
- The situation changes before the end.
- The ending creates a next-step question or emotional handoff.

Failure signs:

- The chapter only explains.
- Characters talk without changing anything.
- The ending simply stops.
- The reader cannot say what this chapter accomplished.

## Character Gate

Pass when:

- Character actions fit known traits and pressure.
- Reactions are specific, not generic.
- Dialogue reflects status, desire, fear, or strategy.
- Major choices change the external situation.

Failure signs:

- Everyone speaks with the same voice.
- The character changes attitude because the plot needs it.
- Emotion is stated but not acted.
- A major event receives no reaction.

## Conflict Gate

Pass when conflict includes:

- desire
- obstruction
- pressure
- choice
- cost
- consequence

Conflict can be external, social, emotional, moral, informational, or time-based.

Failure signs:

- Conflict is only noise or argument.
- The outcome would be the same regardless of character action.
- The obstacle is solved too easily.

## Pacing Gate

Pass when:

- Each scene has a function.
- Exposition appears where pressure makes it relevant.
- The chapter alternates action, reaction, information, and decision.
- Paragraph length supports readability.

Failure signs:

- Long backstory before reader investment.
- Repeated internal summaries.
- No information gain for many paragraphs.
- Too many scene goals at once.

## Web-Novel Retention Gate

Pass when:

- The opening gives reader orientation quickly.
- The type promise is visible.
- The chapter has a small reward.
- A new question, danger, or desire remains.
- The next chapter feels necessary.

Failure signs:

- Hook is vague.
- The chapter pays off nothing.
- All problems are solved too cleanly.
- The reader has no reason to continue.

## Revision Gate

Before Draft B, ensure:

- user feedback is classified
- must-change items are addressed
- approved sections are preserved
- new contradictions are checked
- changes are summarized

## Finalization Gate

Before Draft C is accepted:

- plot unchanged unless requested
- canon unchanged unless requested
- dialogue less expository
- emotions embodied
- generic phrasing reduced
- final continuity record updated

## Diagnostic Questions

When a draft feels weak, ask:

- What does the character want in this scene?
- What blocks them?
- What changes by the end?
- What does the reader learn or feel?
- What promise is being delayed?
- What would make the choice harder?
- What detail would make the scene visible?
